Mastery

Three years ago, I read a book called Mastery by American Author Robert Greene. This ended up changing my whole outlook on life. I have been more productive and outgoing ever since then. Now this might not mean that the book changed me completely, but it is important to understand how the book makes readers open their brain to endless possibilities.

Every man holds his fortune in his own hands, like a sculptor the raw material he will fashion into a figure. But it's the same with that type of artistic activity as with all others: We are merely born with the capability to do it. The skill to mold the material into what we want must be learned and attentively cultivated.

-JOHAN WOLFGANG GOETHE

Green calls this skill, Mastery. Over the centuries, people have placed a wall around such mastery. It has been given many names..genius, wizardly and even cursed. It was considered to be achieved either through birth or by some sort of witchcraft. But this wall doesnt exist. The brain that we possess is the work of 6 million years of development, and more than anything else, this evolution of the brain was designed to lead us to mastert, the latent power within us all.

The book mentions various characters that the author considers "Masters" of their fields. And while reading I have found these people somewhat inspirational as their stories of development is worth learning from.
